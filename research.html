Current Research

Techno-Economic Analysis of Energy Recovery Pathways for Single-Use Plastic Wastes

Tasfia Ferdoush, Department of Chemical Engineering, BUET
Supervisor: Dr. Kawnish Kirtania
  • Performed detailed characterization of single-use plastic waste, including elemental (CHNS/O) analysis and higher heating value (HHV) measurements to evaluate feedstock composition and energy potential.
  • Developed a comprehensive process simulation for plastic waste recovery using Aspen PLUS, integrating conventional thermal conversion technologies with novel process enhancements to optimize energy and material recovery.
  • Conducted techno-economic analysis based on Aspen PLUS simulation outputs to assess the feasibility, cost-effectiveness, and scalability of the proposed recovery system.

Past Research

Life Cycle and Techno-Economic Analysis of Multi-Layer Packaging Recycling Supply Chain for Policy Recommendation

Tasfia Ferdoush, Department of Chemical Engineering, BUET
Supervisor: Dr. Kawnish Kirtania, Dr. Kazi Bayzid Kabir
  • Designed a circular and sustainable material flow model for multi-layered packaging (MLP) waste by integrating both conventional and emerging plastic recovery technologies.
  • Performed life cycle assessment (LCA) and techno-economic analysis of the proposed MLP recovery system using Aspen PLUS simulations to evaluate environmental impact and economic viability.
  • Formulated evidence-based policy recommendations, including the adoption of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) frameworks, informed by Key Informant Interviews (KII) and field-level data collection.

Undergraduate Thesis

Fertilizer Potential of Landfill Municipal Solid Waste

Tasfia Ferdoush, Department of Chemical Engineering, BUET
Supervisor: Dr. Syeda Sultana Razia
  • Assessed feasibility of using municipal landfill organic solid waste as a fertilizer based on physical and chemical properties, compost maturity parameter, and germination index (GI).
  • Analyzed real municipal landfill solid waste samples using UV-Vis Spectroscopy, Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy, Cold Vapor Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy, and CHNS analysis.
  • The maturity parameters- NPK ratio, germination index (GI) tested for radish seeds, macro and micronutrient concentrations, heavy metal concentrations, and C: N ratio suggested that the samples were mature enough to be used as organic fertilizer.
